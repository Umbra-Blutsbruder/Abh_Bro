The Loss Of The Thinking Man: Chapter 1

In the Western world, a dramatic shift has occurred over the last century. This shift is what I'd like to call: the loss of the Thinking Man. To explain what I mean, let me give a few examples. I was looking at a map of the world with a friend of mine. When we began to look at Asia, he stated that while he wasn't sure about the locations of any of the Asian nations, he was quite certain he was pointing at Korea. He was, in fact, pointing at Vietnam. This shows a lack of understanding of not only the location of Korea, but a complete lack of understanding of which nations border Korea and where they might be located.

At a different time, with the same friend, I began to talk about the structure of the state. This was a topic that he found quite boring and thusly, he did not engage with it all that much. It appeared to me as if he simply had no interest in how the state was run. About a half hour later, he was practically bouncing off the walls with excitement. Why? Because his favourite football team had gotten rid of their coach who many blamed for their bad performance. This man showed no interest in what would affect him for his entire life, and yet would not stop talking about which man should lead a football team he likes.

These examples do not serve to enrich my ego, or make my friend seem foolish. It is to show through my experiences a phenomenon that many men have been going through across the West. We've began to lose a cultural standard we used to hold our men to, the standard to keep our men well-educated and in the habit of thinking. Our men are perfectly capable of such thinking, listen to any conversation men have about strategies football teams should employ or about the benefits of a certain type of engine over another and you will find the seeds of a mind that, if cultivated, could grow into a learned and knowledgeable mind. And yet, our men spend very little to no time studying matters which are genuinely important.

I request the reader to do an experiment. Ask a man which news channel they gather their information from, then ask them about politics. I can guarantee that most of the time, the reader will find that the man will simply state what the aforementioned news channel claims. These men have surrendered their thinking, opting to simply download the current cultural norms from social media and news networks. In essence, these men have given up the idea of thinking too hard about, well, anything.

To spell it out as plainly as possible, my point is this: we've lost the idea of the 'thinking man'. We no longer have a sizeable population of men who ponder politics, philosophy or any other topic worth pondering. Instead, we have now found ourselves surrounded by the 'parrot man', men who simply repeat what they hear, often without even knowing why they believe what they believe. This is having drastically negative effects on Christendom and if it isn't solved soon, could have even worse consequences down the line.

Let me list out a couple questions that I wish for the reader to ponder before I give my own response. Firstly, ponder what kind of effect the loss of the Thinking Man has on politics. What happens when a large section of the population simply believes what they are told to believe online and through the media. Secondly, ponder what kind of effect this is having on romance. What a woman must feel when she finds herself surrounded by men who, quite submissively, parrot the current narrative. And lastly, ponder what effect this is having on our culture.

To start off with, in a brief summary, our politics is impacted to such an extent that to call any Western nation a "democracy" has become quite foolish. By Aristotle's definition, 'rule of the many' still applies. However, if you think about it in the context that most men have been taught not to question the narrative they grew up in, one must ponder whether the many are in control, or if it is merely the few oligarchs who decide what the current narrative is who are in control. Without properly being informed, one cannot consent. And thus, I'd argue that due to the lack of information being given to our men and also the culture around men which makes them disregard the habit of thinking and studying, we cannot call the nations of the West democracies as per the 'rule of the many' definition. Due to the loss of the Thinking Man, the Western world has descended into oligarchies. A simple look at our politics shows this fact quite clearly. Debates use to take hours, with each side clearly laying out their arguments. Now, "debates" have turned into duels of covert insults lacking any true intellectual value. Debates are simply entertainment now, not an event where people are genuinely expected to think about their own views. News networks have also sunk down into the habit of merely attacking the enemies, much like a drama show with distinct characters with narrative arcs. Elections too have simply became a game about 'who can say the best slogans'. All this combined strengthens my oligarch theory. Nothing about politics genuinely attempts to change your mind or make you think, you're not supposed to. You are simply supposed to attach yourself to the ideological pipeline of one of the oligarchs and do as he says. You are not supposed to exercise any thought, you are supposed to be a puppet for an oligarch. I'd say the loss of the Thinking Man was the primary reason the democratic system fell and the oligarch system came forth. Simply put, the many cannot lead if the many do not think about how to lead. Thus, they surrender leading to the few who still can, while they live comfortable and distracted lives. The oligarchs gain supreme power over the nation, and in return, the many gain a nation which, in the short term, provides everything they could ever need without having to ask for it.

Next, I'd like to talk about the effect the loss of the Thinking Man is having on romance. It is clear that beauty is subjective. To a man, a woman's beauty is generally revealed through her form. When thinking of a beautiful woman, a man thinks of hips that can provide many children, hair that shows good health and features that show good inheritable genes. In my personal opinion, after thinking of the topic for some time, this is due to the fact that the woman is the one responsible for carrying the child into the world. Health is shown on the body, and thus it makes evolutionary sense for men to be attracted to women who clearly show that they can handle the physical burden of pregnancy by possessing a healthy body. To put it simply, the baby will be as healthy as the woman's body, thus men look for healthy looking women. This is not the case in reverse. A woman will not find a man more attractive the less clothing he has on. Put a man wearing nothing but shorts next to a man with a suit on and women will generally find the man in the suit more attractive. This is because the man is not the one burdened with carrying the child, and thus the child will not be effected by the health of the male's body nearly as much as he would with the woman's. Thus, women do not concern themselves nearly as much with physical traits and instead focus on the male's ability to provide. This makes sense once more as when the woman is with child, she will find herself more and more in need of the man's help to provide sustenance for herself and any child who is not yet ready for independence. Thus, a man becomes more attractive the better he can provide due to evolutionary reasons. To simplify everything I just said into a sentence: A woman's beauty is her fertility and health, a man's beauty is his strength in action. This relates to the loss of the Thinking Man for an important reason, what happens when the men lose the ability to ponder abstractly. On the surface, this seems irrelevant. However, to provide well, one must be intelligent. To not waste your strength, you must be able to think abstractly. Women have relied on the logic of men since the start of humanity, what will women think when one of the most powerful tools men use to provide falls away? Subconsciously, unless mentally unwell, women look for men who can provide for them for the aforementioned reason I just gave, what occurs when a woman finds a man who simply has no drive to do better, learn more, improve their mind? I will most likely ponder this question for a while before I write an official conclusion to it in my later chapters, for now I can only make vague predictions. However, I humbly believe and ask anyone who knows better to correct me in due time, think that this is having a negative effect on dating. Women have a good and natural desire to submit to a man, so women will naturally find themselves without a suitable partner if all she can find are men who surrender their capacity to lead their own lives and think of their own ideas to oligarchs. I, also humbly and with a willingness to be corrected, think that this is having a worse effect on women than men (I wish to add that I want to write a chapter about the loss of the Thinking Woman as well, however that is outside the scope of this chapter). I believe this to be the case as men are evolutionarily designed to be fine alone. They have a broad window of fertility and, outside of old age, never find themselves dependant on anyone. This would naturally cause them to not get as strong of a drive for companionship. Women are different, they only have a small window of fertility and during that window, if they have a partner, would naturally find themselves dependent on a man for protection and provisions. This would cause them to develop a stronger natural desire to find a partner as soon as possible to increase the likelihood of her survival and of procreation. Due to this, I think men have developed to be better at handling being lonely, while women are probably worse off being alone as they are most likely psychologically harmed by loneliness the most out of the two genders. To conclude, women find themselves attracted to men who are intelligent and have a habit of improving themselves as those traits are evolutionarily attractive to them, however, they find that most men have given up those abilities and have submitted to a life of comfort and submissiveness. This causes women to have no choice but to isolate themselves from men, harming dating and romance.

As for the last question to ponder in this chapter, I wish to think about the cultural and societal effects the loss of the Thinking Man creates. First, what is culture? To put it simply, it is a collection of habits and practices that a people follow in order to solve issues. A culture makes music as the problem of boredom must be solved. A culture has a certain type of dance circle as unity is best strengthened by both physical contact and shared practices. A culture has a certain dish as that dish provides benefits to their immune system. If you look at any culture, you tend to find traditions running through it that serve the purpose of fixing problems nobody even remembers anymore. Now, what happens when a people trade in their traditions to whatever the media says? To answer this, simply look at our problems. Let us use dating as an example. The usual tradition across Christendom was simple, you'd find a partner through friends, you two went on a couple dates, waited until marriage before bedding each other and were expected to have children. These traditions have been destroyed and what are the results? Marriage rates have plummeted as people cannot seem to find their partners anymore. Most people end up having nothing in common with who they date as when you do not find your partner through your friends, you do not have any guarantee that you two will have anything in common. Despite sex being "liberated", male virginity has risen as without a proper path to married life, there is no guarantee that a man will get to have a woman to call his own. Women are no longer expected to provide the next generation and instead, they are told to focus on themselves and what do we find? The population of the Western world has plummeted, all while women are the most hopeless and depressed they have ever been. All this happened though the loss of the Thinking Man. Any man who studies, ponders and improves himself will know that no matter how hard you try to deny it, the meaning of life is friendship, love and family. No Thinking Man would have ever agreed to our current culture of disgustingly submissive and undisciplined men and whorish women. But they are gone, no Thinking Man is around to set the standard anymore, thus the oligarchs are now free to change culture to whatever they wish, changing it to cultures which do not have the capability to provide us with what makes life worth living. The loss of the Thinking Man resulted in us losing many cultural traditions which all existed in perfect harmony to lead us to the perfect life, centered around family, love and friendship. The Thinking Man was the defender of such traditions, and thus his loss has torn an ugly wound on the culture of the West. Marriage, dating and falling birth rates are just a couple examples, our entire culture has felt the sting of the loss of our drive to learn.

In conclusion, the following chapters will act as explorations of the effects the loss of the Thinking Man is having on Western society. These chapters will also aim to point out to any reader how they can not only pick up the habits that make a Thinking Man, but potentially how they can affect society around them as a whole to repair the damage that has been done to our world.
